% EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
% ============================================
\textit{Forward Deployed Engineering (FDE) has emerged as the defining
organizational innovation at the intersection of enterprise software and
artificial intelligence. Pioneered by Palantir Technologies in the early
2000s---where engineers literally operated at forward military bases and
aboard Navy ships---the model places elite software engineers directly at
customer sites to solve their hardest data and systems problems. What
began as a niche practice for intelligence community work has exploded
into the mainstream: job postings surged +1,165\% year-over-year through
October 2025 (Lightcast), a16z dubbed it ``the hottest job in tech,'' and
companies from OpenAI to Salesforce are scaling dedicated FDE
organizations. The catalyst is clear: as enterprises race to deploy LLMs
and autonomous AI agents, the gap between what AI models can do in the
lab and what they can do inside a Fortune~500's messy data estate has
never been wider. FDEs are the human bridge.}

\vspace{6pt}

\textit{Yet the model carries material risks. Ex-Palantir CFO Colin
Anderson warns that deploying FDEs on contracts below \$10M is ``lighting
equity on fire.'' FDEs cost 25--40\% more than equivalent software
engineers, burnout is endemic (Palantir's work-life balance rated 2.7/5
on Glassdoor), and without platform-first discipline, companies risk
drowning in thousands of bespoke solutions. Barry McCardel, a five-year
Palantir FDE veteran and CEO of Hex, cautions bluntly: ``Companies are
cargo-culting FDE culture. For most: absolutely f***ing not.'' The model
works brilliantly under three conditions---high-value markets (F500 and
government only), epistemological humility about product direction, and
extensible platform architecture---and fails spectacularly without them.
This report provides the definitive analysis of FDE history, economics,
operational models, and strategic implications for technology leaders
evaluating the approach.}

% KEY TAKEAWAYS
% ============================================
\begin{tcolorbox}[
    colback=mslightgray, colframe=msblue,
    boxrule=1pt, arc=3pt,
    left=8pt, right=8pt, top=6pt, bottom=6pt,
    title={\textbf{Key Takeaways}}
]
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Explosive growth}: FDE job postings surged +1,165\%
        YoY (Jan--Oct 2025 vs.\ 2024, Lightcast), with 922 active
        postings in November 2025 and October marking the all-time
        record month.

    \item \textbf{AI is the catalyst}: 35\% of FDE postings now require
        AI agent experience, 31\% require LLM skills; the role is
        evolving from data integration toward autonomous agent deployment
        and operationalization.

    \item \textbf{Severe talent shortage}: 3 open engineering positions
        per qualified candidate (Addison Group, 2026); salary growth at
        4.2\% average, up to 10\% for high-demand AI skills.

    \item \textbf{Economics demand scale}: FDEs cost 25--40\% more than
        equivalent SWEs and only generate positive ROI on contracts above
        \$10M. Median base compensation: \$173,816; top-end Palantir TC
        exceeds \$600K.

    \item \textbf{Platform-first or fail}: Without rigorous feedback
        loops from field to product, FDE programs devolve into expensive
        consulting. ``If FDEs prefer one-offs over your abstraction,
        it's the wrong abstraction'' (McCardel).

    \item \textbf{Proven alumni pipeline}: FDE programs produce
        disproportionately exceptional careers---Anduril (2 co-founders
        ex-FDE), Hex, Veraset, and leadership roles at OpenAI, xAI,
        and Citadel.

    \item \textbf{Evolving toward Agent Deployment Engineer}: As AI
        agents become autonomous, the FDE role is shifting from
        building custom integrations to managing, monitoring, and
        governing deployed AI agents (Celonis AgentC model).
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}
