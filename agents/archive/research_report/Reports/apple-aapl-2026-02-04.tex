\section{Apple Intelligence: The 2026 Catalyst}

Apple's AI strategy represents the most significant platform evolution since the App Store. The company's privacy-first approach differentiates it from competitors but has resulted in delayed feature rollout.

\subsection{LLM Siri Overhaul}

The centerpiece of Apple's AI roadmap is the complete Siri redesign, now targeting Spring 2026 with iOS 26.4:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Architecture Reset}: Craig Federighi acknowledged first-gen architecture was "too limited"; transition to second-generation LLM-based architecture required
    \item \textbf{On-Device Models}: Foundation Models framework provides developers access to 3B parameter on-device LLMs, reducing API costs and latency
    \item \textbf{Hybrid Processing}: Combines local LLMs with selective cloud processing; partnerships expected for third-party models (e.g., custom Gemini for web search)
    \item \textbf{Multi-Turn Conversations}: New Siri will maintain context across interactions, enabling complex task orchestration (calendar, reservations, weather coordination)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Leadership Transition}

Apple restructured its AI organization in late 2025:

\begin{itemize}
    \item John Giannandrea (AI/ML chief) retiring in 2026
    \item \textbf{Amar Subramanya} hired from Google Gemini team --- signals strategic acceleration
    \item AI team larger than previously reported; WWDC 2025 Foundation Models framework indicates robust internal capabilities
\end{itemize}

\subsection{iPhone 17 AI Supercycle Thesis}

Morgan Stanley and other analysts project an AI-driven upgrade supercycle in FY2026-2027:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Apple Intelligence availability correlated with stronger iPhone 16 performance in enabled markets
    \item LLM Siri + enhanced on-device AI features expected to drive replacements from iPhone 12/13 base
    \item Services attach rate expected to increase with AI-powered features requiring iCloud+ and Apple One subscriptions
\end{itemize}
