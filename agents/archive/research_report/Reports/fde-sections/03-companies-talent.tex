\subsection{What Drives the Surge}

The +1,165\% YoY growth in FDE postings is not a hiring fad. Three
structural forces converge:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{The AI deployment gap}: LLMs and AI agents work well
        in demos but require deep integration with enterprise data,
        workflows, and governance to deliver production value. FDEs are
        the integration layer.
    \item \textbf{The trust deficit}: Enterprises spending \$10M+ on AI
        expect hands-on engineering support, not documentation links.
        FDEs build trust through co-creation.
    \item \textbf{The feedback imperative}: AI products improve fastest
        when engineers observe real users in real environments. FDEs are
        the highest-bandwidth feedback channel.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Soft Skills (The True Differentiator)}

The most common hiring mistake is over-indexing on coding ability and
under-testing for the interpersonal capabilities that define FDE
effectiveness:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Customer Fluency}: Ability to translate between
        technical and business language in real time.
    \item \textbf{Grit \& Radical Ownership}: Taking full responsibility
        for outcomes in ambiguous, high-pressure environments.
    \item \textbf{Problem Decomposition}: Breaking ill-defined customer
        problems into tractable engineering tasks.
    \item \textbf{Adaptability}: Context-switching between industries,
        tech stacks, and organizational cultures weekly.
    \item \textbf{Business Acumen}: Understanding ROI, deal economics,
        and customer success metrics.
    \item \textbf{Empathy}: Reading the room---knowing when to push
        technically and when to listen.
\end{itemize}

The canonical FDE description: \textit{``Code like ICs, communicate like
product managers, navigate customers like founders.''}
